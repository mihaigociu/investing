# Contract Mix & AI Exposure — Cognizant

*The single most important structural question for the AI thesis: **how much of Cognizant's revenue is billed by the hour (exposed to AI cutting hours) versus billed for an outcome (where AI productivity helps the vendor)?** This file answers it with the actual disclosure from Note 2 of the FY2025 10-K.*

> **Source:** Cognizant FY2025 Form 10-K, Note 2 (Revenue Recognition — disaggregation by contract type), filed Feb 2026. SEC accession 0001058290-26-000008. Figures in **$ millions**, exact from the filing.

---

## 1. The data — revenue by contract type

| Contract type | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 | 2-yr growth | FY2025 share |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| **Time and materials (T&M)** | 8,888 | 8,926 | 9,149 | **+2.9%** | **43.3%** |
| **Fixed-price** | 8,664 | 9,028 | 10,010 | **+15.5%** | **47.4%** |
| **Transaction- or volume-based** | 1,801 | 1,782 | 1,949 | +8.2% | 9.2% |
| **Total revenue** | **19,353** | **19,736** | **21,108** | +9.1% | 100% |

**Share trend (the key movement):**

| | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| T&M share | 45.9% | 45.2% | **43.3%** ↓ |
| Fixed-price share | 44.8% | 45.7% | **47.4%** ↑ |
| Transaction/volume share | 9.3% | 9.0% | 9.2% → |

> **The headline:** over two years, **fixed-price revenue grew +15.5% while time-and-materials grew just +2.9%.** Fixed-price has overtaken T&M as the largest bucket and is *gaining* share; the pure "bill-by-the-hour" model is *shrinking* as a proportion of the business. This is the opposite of the crude bear caricature that "Cognizant is 100% billable hours about to be gutted by AI."

---

## 2. Why the mix decides whether AI is a headwind or a tailwind

Each contract type reacts to AI-driven productivity in a *completely different direction*:

| Bucket | How it's billed | What AI productivity does to it | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Time & materials** (43%) | Client pays per hour / per person | Fewer hours needed → **fewer billable hours → direct revenue loss.** This is the exposed bucket. | ⚠️ **Headwind** (revenue) |
| **Fixed-price** (47%) | Client pays a fixed amount for a defined outcome | Deliver the same outcome with fewer people → Cognizant **keeps the saving as margin.** | ✅ **Tailwind** (margin) — *if it can hold the price* |
| **Transaction/volume** (9%) | Client pays per transaction / unit of output | Largely **decoupled from labor hours**; can even benefit if volumes grow. | ➖ **Mostly neutral / mild positive** |

**The blunt arithmetic:** only the **~43% of revenue in T&M is directly in AI's crosshairs on the revenue line.** The other **~57% (fixed-price + transaction-based) is either helped or neutral** — for that majority, AI-driven efficiency lowers *Cognizant's cost of delivery*, and if the outcome price holds, the productivity gain accrues to Cognizant as fatter margin, not to a shrinking top line.

> **This is the most important correction to the simple bear thesis.** "AI deflates the labor-arbitrage model" is only unambiguously true for the T&M book. For nearly half the business (fixed-price), the same AI efficiency is a *margin expansion story* — which is consistent with management guiding margins *up* toward ~16% even as growth is questioned. And the mix is drifting the favorable way on its own.

---

## 3. The crucial counter-nuances (why this isn't a green light)

The mix is a genuine mitigant, not an all-clear. Three things stop it from neutralizing the bear case:

1. **Pricing pass-through hits *all* contract types.** Your insider read — clients are *"sticky but price-squeezed"* — is the key qualifier. Fixed-price only protects Cognizant *if it keeps the outcome price.* If clients demand that AI savings be passed back (which the insider confirms is happening), then fixed-price contracts get **repriced downward at renewal**, and the "AI helps margin" benefit is competed away to the client over time. Fixed-price defends you for the *life of the current contract*; it doesn't defend you at *renegotiation.*

2. **The mix shift is partly AI-*driven*, and not purely good.** Clients increasingly *prefer* fixed-price/outcome deals precisely because they no longer want to pay for hours they suspect AI can eliminate. So the shift toward fixed-price is partly the market **forcing** Cognizant off the T&M gravy train — a symptom of eroding pricing power, not just a savvy strategic choice. And fixed-price carries **delivery risk**: bid the effort wrong (easy when AI is changing productivity assumptions monthly) and Cognizant eats the overrun.

3. **T&M is still $9.1B of revenue.** A 43% bucket that stops growing — or starts shrinking — as AI cuts hours is a real drag. If T&M revenue declined even 5%/yr, that's ~$450M/yr of headwind the fixed-price margin story has to outrun.

## 4. Segment exposure — where the T&M risk concentrates (FY2025)

Contract mix isn't uniform across Cognizant's four segments. Splitting the FY2025 disclosure:

| Segment | Revenue | T&M % | Fixed-price % | Transaction % | AI revenue-exposure |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|
| **Health Sciences (HS)** | 6,347 | 31% | 50% | 19% | **Lowest** — most outcome/transaction-based, most resilient |
| **Financial Services (FS)** | 6,173 | **51%** | 46% | 3% | **High** — most T&M-heavy; 2nd-largest segment |
| **Products & Resources (P&R)** | 5,285 | 42% | 51% | 7% | Moderate |
| **Comms/Media/Tech (CMT)** | 3,303 | **54%** | 41% | 5% | **Highest** T&M share (smallest segment) |
| **Total** | 21,108 | 43% | 47% | 9% | |

> **Read:** the risk isn't evenly spread. **Health Sciences — the largest segment — is the most AI-resilient** (only 31% T&M, 19% transaction-based). **Financial Services (2nd-largest) is the most exposed on the revenue line** at 51% T&M. CMT is the most T&M-heavy but smallest. So the revenue most vulnerable to AI hour-compression is concentrated in FS and CMT (~$5.6B combined T&M); HS's mix is a genuine buffer. Watch FS growth closely — it's the canary.

## 5. Supporting: service-line mix (FY2023–2025, $M)

| Service line | FY2023 | FY2024 | FY2025 | 2-yr growth |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Consulting & technology services | 11,964 | 12,492 | 13,533 | **+13.1%** |
| Outsourcing services | 7,389 | 7,244 | 7,575 | +2.5% |

Consulting/technology (the higher-value, project/transformation work — including AI-build demand) is growing ~5x faster than traditional outsourcing. Consistent with the story that new AI-transformation projects are a real demand source offsetting commoditizing run-work — the "tailwind" side of the AI ledger showing up in the numbers.

## 6. What this changes in the thesis

- **Softens the revenue bear case:** only ~43% of revenue (T&M) is directly exposed to AI hour-cutting, and that bucket is already the *slowest-growing* and *losing share*. The reverse-DCF's implied ~3–5%/yr decline looks *less* likely to be driven by a top-line collapse than the simple caricature suggests.
- **Sharpens the margin/pricing question:** the real battleground is whether Cognizant **keeps** the AI productivity gains on its ~$10B fixed-price book, or **hands them back** via lower renewal pricing ("price-squeezed"). That's a *margin* fight, not a *volume* fight — and it's the thing to monitor in the disclosed margin trajectory.
- **Nudges the scorecard:** this is why *long-term prospects* (#9) and *moat trend* (#3) are held at a cautious-but-not-catastrophic level rather than a hard fail. The business has more structural resilience than the sector panic implies — but the pricing-power erosion the insider describes is exactly what would convert this mitigant into a slow bleed.

### What to watch (quarterly)
- **T&M revenue trajectory** — is the 43% bucket flat-to-growing, or does it start declining? (The clearest AI-revenue-deflation signal.)
- **Fixed-price margin** — is Cognizant *keeping* AI savings (margin up) or passing them through (margin flat/down despite efficiency)?
- **FS segment growth** — the most T&M-exposed large segment; the canary for AI hour-compression.
- **Continued mix shift** — if fixed-price keeps gaining share *and* margins hold, that's the bull path; if fixed-price gains share while margins erode, that's pricing pass-through winning (bear).
