**Reading the trend:** a **turnaround interrupted by a sector storm.** After years as a low-growth, margin-lagging laggard (revenue roughly flat 2022→2024, GAAP operating margin sagging to 13.9% in 2023), CEO Ravi Kumar re-accelerated the business: FY2025 revenue **+7% (+6.4% cc)** with four straight organic-growth quarters, GAAP operating margin recovering sharply to **16.1%** (a genuine +240 bps off the 2023 trough), and adjusted EPS **+11%**. On the fundamentals alone, 2025 was Cognizant's best year in some time — which makes the ~48% stock collapse in 2026 a story about *sentiment and the sector's future*, not a company that missed.

> ⚠️ **Watch the GAAP net-income line — it tells a subtler story.** GAAP net income was essentially **flat** (2024 $2,240M → 2025 $2,230M) *despite* operating income jumping **+$497M** (+17%). That means a large **below-the-line item in 2025 — almost certainly a higher effective tax rate or a discrete tax charge (~$500M swing)** — absorbed the entire operating gain. It's why GAAP diluted EPS rose only **+1%** while *adjusted* EPS rose **+11%**. Confirm the exact driver in the 10-K tax footnote; treat 2025 GAAP net income as tax-depressed and 2024 as possibly tax-flattered when normalizing.

> ✅ **SBC is genuinely low — a real quality point (and a correction to earlier estimates).** Actual SBC is only **~$175–181M/yr, ~0.9% of revenue** — *not* the ~$300M first assumed. The adjusted-vs-GAAP EPS gap ($5.28 vs $4.56 ≈ $0.72) is therefore driven more by intangible amortization and the 2025 tax item than by stock comp. Cognizant "adjusts" far less aggressively than most tech/IT names (and light-years cleaner than SPAC-era Rover, whose SBC ran ~10% of revenue). Still: use GAAP and *subtract SBC* for owner earnings.

> **Revenue by contract type (T&M vs. fixed-price) and by segment/service line is analyzed in its own file — see `contract-mix-and-ai-exposure.md`.** It's the most important structural cut for the AI thesis: only ~43% of revenue (time-and-materials) is directly exposed to AI cutting billable hours; ~47% (fixed-price, and growing) is a *margin* story instead.

**Reading it:** a **book-to-bill of ~1.3x** means Cognizant booked $1.30 of future contract value for every $1.00 of revenue recognized — backlog is growing faster than revenue, historically a positive forward signal. One quarter in 2026 reportedly saw bookings **+21%**. This is the strongest *bull* data point: demand, as measured by what clients are actually signing, was still healthy into 2026. **The bear rebuttal:** bookings are total contract value over multi-year terms and can mask *pricing* erosion (more scope at lower rates) and don't yet reflect AI-driven deflation that shows up later. Watch whether book-to-bill stays ≥1.0.
