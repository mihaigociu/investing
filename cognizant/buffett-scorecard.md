**Sub-scores that tell the story:**
- **Business quality (items 1–9): 28/45 → mediocre-to-fair.** A capital-light, cash-rich, competently-run-lately business with a *narrow, eroding moat* and a *flagged* management. Not a "wonderful business."
- **Investment attractiveness at $43 (item 10): 3/5 → cheap but not a bargain.** The price is low; the margin of safety is thin for the risk.

## Verdict

- **Is CTSH a *wonderful business*?** **No.** It's a decent, cash-generative business with a narrow and eroding moat, historically a sector laggard, run by a management you (the insider) don't fully trust. It fails the "forever-hold, widening-moat" Buffett ideal.
- **Is it a *value trap* or a *bargain* at ~$43?** **Undetermined — and that's the honest answer.** The reverse-DCF says the market already prices gentle decline; you win if the business merely stagnates, lose if AI drives faster decline. The insider evidence ("already cannibalizing," "price-squeezed," "extracting") tilts toward caution but is not conclusive.
- **Intrinsic value range:** **~$30 (bear) to ~$75 (bull), base ~$52.** The width is real, not a hedge — the AI question is unresolved.
- **My buy price (35–40% margin of safety on ~$52 base):** **~$32–34.**
- **The ~$43 verdict:** **cheap but not yet safe — WAIT / buy only small.** Not a "back up the truck." Not an "avoid" either.

## Bottom line (plainly)

> Cognizant at ~$43 is the mirror image of Rover at $11. Rover was a *wonderful business at a rich price* — great company, bad investment. Cognizant is a *fair business at a cheap price* — and Buffett's warning is that "a fair company at a wonderful price" is still not the goal, and can be a value trap if the fairness is fading. The reverse-DCF is oddly reassuring: you don't need growth, only decline milder than ~4%/yr. But the three things that would make me size up — a durable moat, trustworthy management, and evidence AI is a net tailwind — are exactly the three the analysis (and your inside view) flags as weak. So: **wait for the low $30s, or wait for two-to-three quarters proving the revenue line survives the AI transition, and buy only a starter before then.** And size it small regardless, because your paycheck is already a large, undiversified bet on the same company.
