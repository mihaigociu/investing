# Rover.com — Value Investing Analysis

A Warren Buffett–style value investing analysis of **Rover Group, Inc.** (formerly Nasdaq: `ROVR`).

## ⚠️ Read this first: Rover is no longer publicly traded

**Blackstone took Rover private on 27 February 2024** in an all-cash deal at **$11.00/share (~$2.3B equity value)**. The stock was delisted from Nasdaq. You cannot buy Rover shares on the public market today.

Because of that, this analysis is framed as a **retrospective case study**:
1. Run the full Buffett-style analysis on Rover using its public-era filings (2021–2023).
2. Arrive at *your own* estimate of intrinsic value.
3. **Grade it against reality** — was Blackstone's $11 a bargain, fair, or rich? What would a value investor have concluded *before* the deal was announced (29 Nov 2023)?

This is arguably the *best* way to learn the method: you get a real, sophisticated buyer's price to check your work against.

## How to use this

Work the phases **in order** in `value-investing-analysis-plan.md`. Phases 1–3 are qualitative (can you understand it? does it have a moat? is management honest and good at capital allocation?). Phases 4–5 are quantitative. Phase 6 is the pre-mortem. Phase 7 is the verdict.

Pull raw numbers using `data-sources-and-checklist.md`, drop them into `valuation-worksheet.md`, and record your final judgment in `buffett-scorecard.md`.

> **Guiding principle (Buffett):** "It's far better to buy a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair company at a wonderful price." First decide if Rover is *wonderful*. Only then argue about price.
