### Valuation multiples at $11.00

| Multiple | At $11 / ~$2.07B EV | Comment |
|---|---:|---|
| EV / Revenue (FY2023E ~$231M) | **~9.0x** | Rich on an absolute basis; reflects ~30%+ growth + margin ramp |
| EV / Adj. EBITDA (FY2023E ~$47M) | **~44x** | High — priced for continued margin expansion |
| EV / est. owner earnings (~$15–20M) | **~100x+** | Very high on *current* cash flow |
| P / E (equity $2.3B ÷ est. net income) | **~100x+** | A growth multiple, not a value multiple |

**The headline finding (preview of the verdict):** On *current* cash generation, $11 was **not a value price** — it was a growth price. It only makes sense if you underwrite years of continued ~20–30% growth *and* Adjusted-EBITDA margins expanding toward 25–30%+ (marketplace maturity). That's exactly the bet a PE buyer like Blackstone makes; a strict Graham/Buffett value investor demanding a margin of safety on *today's* earnings would likely have found $11 too dear — and would have been more interested in the stock during its 2022 lows (~$3–5), when the same business traded far cheaper.
